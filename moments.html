The Flu Game (1997)

In Game 5 of the 1997 NBA Finals, Michael Jordan delivered one of the greatest performances in sports history. Despite being severely ill, he scored 38 points and led the Chicago Bulls to a crucial victory over the Utah Jazz.

Jordan was visibly exhausted throughout the game, often leaning on teammates during timeouts. His performance became legendary, showing toughness, determination, and leadership at the highest level.

Jordan hit a clutch three-pointer late in the game and finished with 38 points, despite barely having the strength to stand.
