The Flu Game (1997)

In Game 5 of the 1997 NBA Finals, Michael Jordan delivered one of the greatest performances in sports history. Even though he was very sick, he scored 38 points and helped lead the Chicago Bulls to a huge win over the Utah Jazz.

Jordan looked exhausted during the game and was even seen leaning on his teammates during timeouts. This moment became legendary because it showed his toughness, leadership, and ability to perform under pressure.

Jordan hit a clutch three-pointer late in the game and finished with 38 points, even though he barely had the strength to stand.
